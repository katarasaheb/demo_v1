Canada's Grocery System is Broken

Canadian grocery shopping is expensive, inefficient, and monopolized. The Competition Bureau Report reveals disturbing trends in our food supply chain.

The 2023 Canadian Competition Bureau Report revealed that the grocery sector is dominated by just three companies, limiting consumer choice and keeping prices high.

  • Monopolized Market: 3 companies control 75% of the grocery market, keeping prices artificially high and limiting consumer choice.
  • Bloated Supply Chains: Inefficient distribution channels lead to unnecessary costs and significant food waste across the system.
  • US Import Dependency: Heavy reliance on US imports makes Canadian groceries vulnerable to tariffs and supply disruptions.
  • Outdated Supermarkets: Traditional stores have high operational costs that are ultimately passed on to consumers.

Result? Canadian households pay more while grocery giants make record profits.
